Lesson 2 (2020-02-10)

Third Person (Singular)

The word ta1 is used for the third person singular both masculine and feminine. However, the corresponding symbol depends depends on the person under consideration:

  • 他: he
  • 她： she

Plural Forms

Adding men (们) takes a word from singular to plural:

  • ni3men: you (plural)
  • wo3men: we
  • ta1men: they

Lesson 3 (2020-02-17)

Correctness of a Statement

Today we learnt how to say whether a statement is correct or not:

  • dui4 means "correct"
  • bu1 dui4 means "not correct"

Note that the tone of bu4 (不) changes to bu1 because the next word (dui4) uses the same tone and consists of only one syllable. This facilitates the pronounciation.

Talking About Temperature

SymbolPinyinTranslation
热re4hot

天tian1day/sky

冬天dong1 tian1winter
大da4big

谢谢xie4 xieThanks.
bu2 ke4 qiYou are welcome.

The symbol 冬 (dong1) on its own also means winter, but in modern Chinese it is common to use words with 2 or 3 syllables.

We can combine what we learnt so far to make statements about the temperature in a city, e.g.:

  • Han4 bao3 re4 ma? (Is it hot in Hamburg)
  • Mun4 ni2 hei1 dong1 tian hen3 leng3. (It is very cold in Munich during the winter.)

or to say that some feels cold/warm, e.g:

  • Ni3 leng3 ma? (Are you cold?)
  • Ta1 bu2 re4. (He does not feel hot.), note the change from bu4 to bu2 (see note above).

Rules to Draw Symbols

We briefly discussed the basic rules in which order to draw the lines in symbols. These rules only provide a rough orientation and there exist numerous exceptions. However, these rules are:

  1. from top to bottom: 三
  2. from left to right: 人
  3. left vertical line usually before upper horizontal stroke: 口
  4. low horizontal line usually last: 王
  5. central vertical line before sides: 小
  6. horizontal before vertical in crossings: 十
  7. lines that slope down to the left before lines that slode down to the right: 文
  8. dots often last: 玉

Lesson 4 (2020-02-22)

We started our first lesson on a Saturday learning "Zao3 shang hao3!" which translates to "Good morning!" where "zao3" means "morning".

Talking About the Weather

We learned the word "tian1 qi4" (天气) which means weather where in day-to-day use "气" is often omitted when making a statement about how the weather is:

  • 天（气）好: The weather is good.
  • 天（气）冷: It is cold.
  • 天（气）热: It is hot/warm.

Talking About How Busy You Are

The word "mang2" (忙) means busy. It is important to note Chinese cultural aspects here. If you are talking about yourself to someone who is not a close friend, you should express modesty. For this purpose the term "xia1 mang2" (瞎忙) exists which expresses that you are busy but with unimportant/minor things. So you could say:

  • Wo3 hen3 mang2, xia1 mang2. (我很忙，瞎忙。)
  • Wo3 xia1 mang2. (我瞎忙。)

Country Names in Chinese

The word "guo2" (国) means country. We learned two examples:

  • zhong1 guo2 (中国): China
  • de2 guo2 (德国): Germany

Lesson 5 (2020-02-24)

Components of the Symbol 我

Today, we learned that the symbol 我 (wo3) is made up of the two components 手 (shou3 - hand) and 戈 (ge3 - spear). Then, we analysed the symbols 意 and 道 which are composed in a similar fashion. The word 意 (yi4 - idea) consists of 心 (xin1 - heart) and 音 （yin1 - tone/sound). The word 道 (dao4 - way) consists of ⻌ and 首 (shou3 - head). Interestingly, 手 and 首 have the same pronounciation. ⻌ is a simplified component of 辵 or ⻍.

German and Chinese

After learning the words for Germany and China last time, we learned the words for Chinese/German:

  • 德语: de2 yu3 - German
  • 德文: de2 wen1 - German
  • 中文: zhong1 wen2 - Chinese

Yesterday, Today, Tomorrow

We learned how to make statements about tomorrow, today and yesterday using the corresponding words:

  • 昨天: zuo2 tian1 - yesterday
  • 今天: jin1 tian1 - today
  • 明天: ming2 tian1 - tomorrow

These words (as well as other time specifications) appear at the beginning of sentences or before the predicate:

  • 我今天很好。
  • 今天我很好。

We also learned some new radicals/components that appear in theses words:

  • 日： ri4 - sun
  • 月: yue - moon
  • 明: ming - bright
